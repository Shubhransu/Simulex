Study of AC Bridges
Aim Theory Procedure Simulator Observation References

Aim

To study and calculate unknown inductor and capacitor using different AC bridges.

Theory

INTRODUCTION

There are three AC bridge circuits which can be realized with the instrument in this experiment. These are the Maxwell bridge, the De Sauty bridge and the Maxwell-Wien bridge.

Figure-1: Wheatstone bridge drawn to accord with the nomenclature on the AC Bridge circuit.

An AC bridge is a Wheatstone bridge with impedances connected in the four arms as shown in Figure-1. The figure is drawn to accord with the symbols on the AC Bridge circuit. SGin and SGout are the two terminals to which the signal generator is to be connected. The terminals marked.A and B are shorted with SGin, while the terminal C is shorted with SGout. Between A and D1 is the impedance Z1. Between D1 and D2 is the DMM in the AC 200 mV range. In the front panel of the bridge, D1 and D2 are connected to two terminals marked DMM. The DMM is to be connected between these two terminals. One can connect any impedance between A and D1. For example, one terminal of a coil is connected to D1 internally and the other end of the coil is connected to a free terminal marked L1. If the terminal A is connected to the terminal marked L1, the coil takes the place of Z1 in Figure-1. In this fashion, by connecting A to the terminals marked L1, L1', L1" three different inductances can be connected as Z1.

Similarly by connecting A to the terminals marked C1, C1' or C1", one may connect three different capacitances as Z1. In the third arm of the bridge circuit, one end of the impedance Z3 is connected internally to D2. By connecting the free end of Z3 to the terminal marked B, one may connect the impedance Z3 in the third arm of the bridge. In the third arm, we may connect inductances L3 or L3', capacitances C3 or C3' and resistances R3 or R3'. Similarly by connecting the terminal marked C to L4, L4', C4, C4' or R4, R4', one may connect an inductance, capacitance or resistance as the impedance Z4. The arm Z2 is a variable resistor (pot). This pot is connected between D1 and C when the switches SW1 and SW2 are pressed down. When they are up, the pot is isolated and its resistance can be measured by connecting a DMM between the terminals marked T1 and T2.

Figure-2: Circuit diagram of AC Bridge circuit.

Maxwell Bridge

In Maxwell bridge, one compares the self-inductances of two coils L1 and L3. These coils are connected as shown in Figure-3.

Figure-3: Maxwell Bridge.

The balancing condition is

$$ \boldsymbol { \frac {\omega L_1} {\omega L_3}= \frac {L_1} {L_3}=\frac {R_2} {R_4}}\ $$

De Sauty Bridge

In De Sauty bridge, one compares the capacitances of two condensers C1 and C3. These condensers are connected as shown in Figure-4.

Figure-4: De Sauty Bridge.

The balancing condition is

$$ \boldsymbol { \frac {1} {\omega C_1}/\frac {1} {\omega C_3}= \frac {C_3} {C_1}=\frac {R_2} {R_4}}\ $$

Maxwell-Wien Bridge

In Maxwell-Wien bridge,one compares the self-inductance of a coil L1 with the capacitance of a condenser C4. The coil and condenser are connected as shown in Figure-5.

Figure-5: Maxwell-Wien Bridge.

The balancing condition is

$$ \frac {j\omega L_1} {R_3}= \frac {R_2} {(1/j\omega C_4)} \\ \\ \boldsymbol {\Rightarrow L_1=C_4R_2R_3}\ $$

Procedure

  1. Rotate the frequency and amplitude konb on the Signal Generator(SG) to adjust the respective values. Press F/V button to toggle display of frequency and amplitude values.
  2. Adjust frequency to be about 5 kHz and set an output voltage between 0.5 to 1 V rms i.e. 1.41 to 2.82 V amplitude on Signal Generator(SG). After setting required parameters on SG, Connect SG button will be enabled. Click on it to connect the SG to AC Bridge Instrument with connector wire.
  3. Select bridge name you want to perform experiment, from drop down menu placed on top left part of control panel.

For Maxwell Bridge

  1. Connect the terminal A to terminal L1, terminal B to terminal L3, and terminal C to terminal R4 on the box by selecting it from control panel placed on top part.
  2. Put the switches S1 and S2 on the box down. Connect a multimeter in the AC millivolts range to the terminals marked DMM on the box. Adjust the potentiometer knob R2 by rotating it till the reading on the multimeter is a minimum. Then, put S1 and S2 up, then Connect DMM button placed just below the 2nd DMM will be enabled and measure the resistance R2 between the terminals T1 and T2 with a different multimeter by clicking on Connect DMM button.
  3. Click on Add button to add reading on Observation table placed below the experimental setup. Any time to clear the table click on Clear button.
  4. To perform another Observation click on Disconnect DMM button then put switches S1 and S2 in down position.
  5. Then, connect terminal C to the terminal marked R4 and repeat the operations. Then, connect terminal B to terminal L3' and repeat the observations, connecting C to R4 and R4' successively. Then, connect A successively to L1' and L1" and repeat the observations.
  6. While balancing the bridge, the multimeter to measure resistance R2 should not be connected to the terminals T1 and T2.
  7. The value of L1, L1' and L1" will be calculated in accodance with balancing condition and will be shown on table. Avarage value of L1, L1' and L1" will be considerd as the required value.

For De Sauty Bridge

  1. Connect the terminal A to terminal marked C1, terminal B to terminal marked C3 and terminal C to terminal marked R4 on the box by selecting it from control panel placed on top part.
  2. Put the switches S1 and S2 on the box down. Connect a multimeter in the AC millivolts range to the terminals marked DMM on the box. Adjust the potentiometer knob R2 by rotating it till the reading on the multimeter is a minimum. Then, put S1 and S2 up, then Connect DMM button placed just below the 2nd DMM will be enabled and measure the resistance R2 between the terminals T1 and T2 with a different multimeter by clicking on Connect DMM button.
  3. Click on Add button to add reading on Observation table placed below the experimental setup. Any time to clear the table click on Clear button.
  4. To perform another Observation click on Disconnect DMM button then put switches S1 and S2 in down position.
  5. Then, connect terminal C to terminal marked R'4, and repeat the operations. Then, connect terminal marked B to terminal marked C3', and repeat the operations. Then, connect A successively to terminals C1' and C1" and repeat the operations.
  6. While balancing the bridge, the multimeter to measure resistance R2 should not be connected to the terminals T1 and T2.
  7. The value of C1, C1' and C1" will be calculated in accodance with balancing condition and will be shown on table. Avarage value of C1, C1' and C1" will be considerd as the required value.

For Maxwell-Wien Bridge

  1. Enter avarage values of L1, L1' and L1" from Maxwell Bridge experimet data in the respective input fields placed just above the Observation table and click on Set Value button to set these values. You can change these values by clicking Change Value button and again set these values.
  2. Connect the terminal A to terminal marked L1, terminal B to terminal marked R3 and terminal C to terminal marked C4 on the box by selecting it from control panel placed on top part.
  3. Put the switches S1 and S2 on the box down. Connect a multimeter in the AC millivolts range to the terminals marked DMM on the box. Adjust the potentiometer knob R2 by rotating it till the reading on the multimeter is a minimum. Then, put S1 and S2 up, then Connect DMM button placed just below the 2nd DMM will be enabled and measure the resistance R2 between the terminals T1 and T2 with a different multimeter by clicking on Connect DMM button.
  4. Click on Add button to add reading on Observation table placed below the experimental setup. Any time to clear the table click on Clear button.
  5. To perform another Observation click on Disconnect DMM button then put switches S1 and S2 in down position.
  6. Then, connect B to terminal marked R3' and repeat the operations. Then connect A successively to terminals marked L1' and L1" and repeat the operations. Then connect C to terminal C4' and repeat the operations.
  7. While balancing the bridge, the multimeter to measure resistance R2 should not be connected to the terminals T1 and T2.
  8. The value of C4 and C4' will be calculated in accodance with balancing condition and will be shown on table. Avarage value of C4 and C4' will be considerd as the required value.
